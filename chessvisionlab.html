Why Research Matters

Chess masters see patterns—not individual pieces—thanks to chunking. Cognitive‑science studies (starting with De Groot, 1965) show that strong players encode typical positions in long‑term memory, letting them recognise ideas instantly. Good news: pattern memory and visualisation are trainable like any other skill.
